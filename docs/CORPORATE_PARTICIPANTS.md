## Ethical Questionnaire for Companies

Before a company can participate in the MATRIX project, a representative must 
answer the following questions. The Ethics Committee reviews responses within 
2 weeks.

### Section 1: Core Business

1. What is your company's primary business?
2. Does your company produce, sell, or support:
   - [ ] Weapons or military equipment
   - [ ] Surveillance systems for authoritarian regimes
   - [ ] Systems designed to deceive, manipulate, or exploit individuals
   - [ ] Fossil fuel extraction or infrastructure
3. Does your company have an ethics policy? (Please attach or link)

### Section 2: AI Ethics

4. How does your company ensure AI systems are safe and interpretable?
5. Does your company use or develop autonomous weapons systems (LAWS)?
6. Does your company collect and sell user data without explicit consent?

### Section 3: Open Source Commitment

7. Has your company contributed to open-source projects before? (List)
8. Will your contributions to MATRIX remain open-source under AGPLv3?
9. Does your company have any patents that could restrict MATRIX usage?

### Section 4: Participation Intent

10. What type of participation are you seeking?
    - [ ] Sponsor (financial support)
    - [ ] Contributor (code, documentation, testing)
    - [ ] User (deployment in production)
    - [ ] Partner (joint development)
11. What do you hope to gain from participating in MATRIX?
12. What resources (time, money, hardware) can you contribute?

## Review Process

1. Company submits questionnaire via GitHub issue (label `corporate-audit`)
2. Ethics Committee reviews (2 weeks)
3. Possible outcomes:
   - **Approved** — added to registry, can contribute immediately
   - **Conditional** — specific concerns to address
   - **Rejected** — violations of Three Prohibitions or incompatibility with AGPLv3 ethics
4. Annual re-review for all corporate participants

## Transparency Commitment

- All approved corporate participants are listed in this file
- Contribution amounts (for sponsors) are public
- No exclusive rights, special access, or closed-door deals
- The community can propose revocation of corporate status via RFC
